## Cost Continues to Rise
### Last Time it was GitHub Copilot, now it's Gemini 3.5 Flash

- Google made Gemini 3.5 Flash its GA agentic and coding model with a 1M token context window, 65K max output, and preserved thoughts across turns. <sup><a href="#appendix-a01">A01</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a02">A02</a></sup>
- Paid pricing is $1.50 per 1M input tokens and $9 per 1M output tokens, versus $0.50 and $3 for Gemini 3 Flash Preview. <sup><a href="#appendix-a01">A01</a></sup>
- Google warns that preserved thoughts increase input token count over multi-turn sessions, which can raise costs unless you clear them for simple queries. <sup><a href="#appendix-a02">A02</a></sup>

---

## Google Wants One Dev Shell

- Google says Gemini CLI and Gemini Code Assist IDE extensions for Gemini Code Assist for individuals, Google AI Pro, and Google AI Ultra will stop serving requests on June 18, 2026, as consumer users are moved to Antigravity CLI. <sup><a href="#appendix-a03">A03</a></sup>
- Google explicitly says there will not be 1:1 feature parity right out of the gate. <sup><a href="#appendix-a03">A03</a></sup>
- GitHub issues filed after the transition announcement include requests for a standalone Gemini CLI path, reports of OAuth quota problems, and an Antigravity CLI startup panic. <sup><a href="#appendix-a04">A04</a></sup>

---

## Cyber Capability Is Accelerating

- The AI Security Institute (AISI) says GPT-5.5 scored 71.4% on expert cyber tasks versus 68.6% for Mythos Preview, making it one of the strongest cyber models it has tested. <sup><a href="#appendix-a05">A05</a></sup>
- AISI says GPT-5.5 was the second model to complete its end-to-end corporate network attack simulation, and its red team identified a universal jailbreak during safeguards testing. <sup><a href="#appendix-a05">A05</a></sup>
- AISI says frontier cyber task horizons are doubling on the order of months, not years. <sup><a href="#appendix-a06">A06</a></sup>

---

## Enterprise Deployment Hardens

- OpenAI and Dell are connecting Codex to Dell AI Data Platform and exploring how it could interface with Dell AI Factory so agents can run closer to governed enterprise data and systems. <sup><a href="#appendix-a07">A07</a></sup>
- OpenAI says more than 4 million developers now use Codex each week. <sup><a href="#appendix-a07">A07</a></sup>
- Anthropic acquired Stainless to deepen SDK generation, CLI tooling, and MCP server connectivity around Claude. <sup><a href="#appendix-a08">A08</a></sup>

---

## AI Rewrites The Org Chart

- Reuters says Meta's May reorganization includes laying off about 10% of staff, moving 7,000 employees into AI initiatives, and flattening management. <sup><a href="#appendix-a09">A09</a></sup>
- Meta's internal memo says many org leaders incorporated AI-native design principles and smaller pods or cohorts into the new structure. <sup><a href="#appendix-a09">A09</a></sup>
- This is what AI adoption looks like when it becomes headcount and workflow strategy, not just a tool rollout. <sup><a href="#appendix-a09">A09</a></sup>

---

## Infrastructure Becomes The Market Story

- Cerebras turned its IPO path into a real May 14 debut, raising $5.55 billion in the largest U.S. IPO of the year so far. <sup><a href="#appendix-a10">A10</a></sup>
- Reuters says demand was driven in part by AI infrastructure enthusiasm plus Cerebras ties to AWS and OpenAI. <sup><a href="#appendix-a10">A10</a></sup>
- Cerebras says Kimi K2.6 is now in enterprise trials and cites an Artificial Analysis measurement of 981 output tokens per second on a private Cerebras endpoint. <sup><a href="#appendix-a11">A11</a></sup>

## What Builders Should Watch

- Model choice is now entangled with token economics, auth path, CLI or agent platform, and where enterprise context actually lives. <sup><a href="#appendix-a01">A01</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a02">A02</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a03">A03</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a07">A07</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a08">A08</a></sup>
- Security teams should assume frontier coding and agent models are becoming materially more capable at vulnerability discovery and attack chaining. <sup><a href="#appendix-a05">A05</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a06">A06</a></sup>
- The real buying question is no longer "which model is best?" but "which stack can we afford, govern, and trust in production?" <sup><a href="#appendix-a01">A01</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a03">A03</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a07">A07</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a08">A08</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a09">A09</a></sup><sup><a href="#appendix-a10">A10</a></sup>
